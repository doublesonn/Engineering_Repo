
This project is intended to develop and strengthen the practical engineering skills required for high-performance circuit design and hardware development. With a strong interest in the application of magnetic energy in real-world systems, this effort focuses on the design and implementation of a high-voltage pulsed power system. The goal is to better understand the generation and measurement of magnetic fields, as well as the practical challenges associated with designing hardware capable of supporting high-energy transient operation.

Initial system requirements have been defined to guide the design process:

- **Peak magnetic field:** 100 mT
- **Capacitor bank voltage:** 500 V
- **Input voltage:** 24 V

In addition to the core power electronics design, the system will incorporate embedded control and sensing. A microcontroller-based platform will be used to monitor system behavior, log performance metrics, and provide safety supervision and state management.
